Basics of Counting

Discrete Mathematical Structures - Unit 1

The basics of counting, also known as combinatorics, involves the study of counting and arranging objects or events in a systematic and organized manner. It provides methods and principles for determining the total number of outcomes, arrangements, or combinations in different situations.

Product Rule

In the basics of counting or combinatorics, the product rule is a fundamental principle used to determine the total number of outcomes or arrangements when multiple independent choices are involved.

To put it in simpler terms, the product rule states that if you have several choices to make independently of each other, and each choice has a certain number of options, then the total number of outcomes or arrangements is found by MULTIPLYING the number of options for each choice together.

The product rule can be used in various situations where you need to calculate the total number of outcomes or arrangements, such as arranging objects, selecting elements from sets, or making choices with different options at each step. It provides a straightforward approach to determining the overall possibilities.Let's have a practical example to further illustrate this concept. Imagine you're going outside and you have few choices to make for your outfit:

  1. You can choose from 4 different shirts.
  2. You have 3 options for pants.
  3. You can select from 2 different pairs of shoes.

By applying the product rule, you multiply the number of options for each choice:
Total number of outfit combinations = 4 * 3 * 2 = 24

Therefore, you have a total of 24 different outfit combinations to choose from.

Sum Rule

The sum rule is another fundamental principle that helps us calculate the total number of outcomes or arrangements when we have multiple MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE choices.

The sum rule states that if there are k mutually exclusive choices to be made, and the first choice has n1 options, the second choice has n2 options, the third choice has n3 options, and so on until the k-th choice has nk options, then the total number of possible outcomes or arrangements is given by the sum:

Total number of outcomes = n1 + n2 + n3 + ... + nk

To better understand the sum rule, let's consider an example. Imagine you're planning a weekend getaway, and you need to decide on the mode of transportation. You have two options: you can either drive or take a bus. Let's break down the possibilities for each choice:

Choice of transportation:
Option 1: Drive
Option 2: Take a bus

Now, let's consider the possibilities for each choice:

Drive:
Option 1: You can take your own car
Option 2: You can rent a car

Take a bus:
Option 1: You can take the economy bus
Option 2: You can take a luxury bus

To determine the total number of transportation options, we apply the sum rule:
Total number of transportation options = Options for driving + Options for taking a bus

Applying the sum rule, we find:
Total number of transportation options = 2 (drive) + 2 (bus) = 4 options

So, you have a total of four transportation options: driving with your own car, driving with a rented car, taking the economy bus, or taking a luxury bus.
