Finite Probability

Discrete Mathematical Structures - Unit 2

Finite probability refers to the likelihood or chance of an event happening within a specific range of possibilities. When we say "finite," it means that there is a limited number of outcomes or options.

To give you a relatable example, let's consider flipping a fair coin. When you flip a coin, there are only two possible outcomes: heads or tails. The probability of getting heads is 1 out of 2, or you can think of it as a 50% chance. The same goes for getting tails—it also has a 50% chance of occurring. Since there are only two options, the probability of either outcome happening is finite.

In simpler terms, finite probability means that the chances of an event occurring are restricted and can be expressed as a fraction or a decimal between 0 and 1. A probability of 0 means the event is impossible, while a probability of 1 means the event is certain to happen.

It's worth noting that finite probability differs from infinite probability. In situations where there are an infinite number of possibilities, like picking a random real number between 0 and 1, the probability of any specific outcome becomes incredibly small and practically negligible.
